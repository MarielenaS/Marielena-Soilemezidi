# msoilemezidi
personal webpage


**Marielena Soilemezidi** is a PhD student at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA); her dissertation focuses on linguistic comprehension and production in agrammatic aphasia. 
Her PhD is funded by the Hellic Foundation for Research and Innovation (H.F.R.I., 3rd Call for doctorate students).

She holds a bachelor’s degree in Philology with a specialization in Linguistics from NKUA and a master’s degree in Cognitive Neuroscience of Language from the University of the Basque Country (UPV) with collaboration with the Basque Center for Cognition, Brani and Language (BCBL). Her master thesis was an eye-tracking study investigating time reference in healthy Spanish speaking adults.

She is also affiliated with the University of Oslo (UiO) working as a research assistant for the project “Morphosyntactic Production in Stroke-induced Agrammatic Aphasia: A Cross-linguistic Machine Learning Approach” of Dr. Valantis Fyndanis. 

Her research interests include acquired language disorders (principally aphasia), and neurobiology of language with a focus on neural correlates of syntactic processing..
